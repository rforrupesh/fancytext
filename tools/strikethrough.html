What is Strikethrough Text?

Strikethrough text, also known as cross-out text, is a typographical effect where a horizontal line is drawn through the center of words. While traditional word processors use an HTML `` or `` tag for this, our generator uses **Unicode combining characters**. This means the strikethrough is part of the actual text character, allowing it to be copied and pasted universally, even in plain text fields where HTML formatting isn't supported.

Why Use Strikethrough Text?

Strikethrough text can serve various purposes and add a unique flair to your digital communication:

  • Showing Edits/Corrections: Ideal for indicating content that has been revised, deleted, or corrected while still keeping the original visible for context.
  • Humor and Sarcasm: Often used to convey irony, sarcasm, or a change of mind in a playful way.
  • "To-Do" Lists: Visually cross out completed tasks in plain text notes or lists.
  • Creative Expression: Stand out on social media profiles, bios, or posts with a distinct text style.
  • Gaming and Chat: Add emphasis or unique formatting in games or chat applications that support Unicode.
