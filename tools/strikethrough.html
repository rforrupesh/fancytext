What Exactly is Strikethrough Text?

At its core, strikethrough text is text with a horizontal line running through its center. You might have seen it in word processors when tracking changes, indicating deleted content, or simply emphasizing a point.

What makes our tool special is that it generates strikethrough using **Unicode combining characters** (specifically, U+0336, the combining long stroke overlay). Unlike HTML tags (`` or ``) which only work on web pages, Unicode strikethrough is part of the character itself. This means your crossed-out text remains formatted when copied and pasted into almost any plain text field, from social media posts to chat apps.

Why Should You Use Strikethrough Text?

The uses for strikethrough text go beyond simple corrections. It's a fantastic way to add personality and specific meaning to your digital communications.

Highlighting Edits and Corrections

This is perhaps the most traditional use. If you're sharing notes, making revisions, or simply want to show a change of thought, striking through old text while keeping it visible provides context. It’s perfect for:

  • **Collaborative documents:** Showing what's been removed but needs to remain visible for discussion.
  • **Correction notes:** Indicating an error in previous communication without completely erasing it.

Marking Completed Tasks

Enhancing Casual Communication
