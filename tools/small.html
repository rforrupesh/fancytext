Choosing Readable Small Text Styles

Small caps offers the easiest reading experience among the three styles. These miniature capital letters maintain recognizable shapes that don't strain your eyes. Use superscript and subscript sparingly for individual words or short phrases rather than full paragraphs.

How Unicode Conversion Works

The converter uses Unicode character replacement to create tiny text. Unicode assigns unique numbers to over 100,000 characters from every language and symbol system. Within this collection exist special letter versions designed for mathematical notation and scientific formulas.

Eye Comfort and Tiny Text

Reading small text doesn't permanently harm your vision but can cause eye fatigue. Your eye muscles work harder focusing on miniature characters, similar to how standing on tiptoes tires leg muscles. Extended reading may cause headaches, dry eyes, and tiredness.

Use tiny text responsibly for short elements like bios or emphasized words. Provide good lighting and take breaks when reading substantial amounts of small text.
