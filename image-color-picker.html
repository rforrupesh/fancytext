What is a Free Color Picker from Image?

A free color picker from image is a simple tool that lets you find any color from a photo in seconds. Instead of guessing shades, you can just upload an image and click on the exact spot to get the real color value. It’s useful for designers, but also for anyone who just wants to know what color they’re looking at.

Upload an Image or Paste a Link

You can start by uploading an image from your device, dragging and dropping it, or even pasting an image URL. This makes it easy to work with both local files and images you find online. If you’re testing different images, the recent history option also helps you quickly go back to them.

Pick Colors by Hovering and Clicking

After your image loads, just move your cursor over it to see colors in real time. When you find something you like, click to lock that color. There’s also a zoom feature that helps you pick colors from very small details without any trouble.

View Color Codes in Different Formats

Once you select a color, the tool shows its values in formats like HEX, RGB, HSL, and more. You don’t need to convert anything yourself. You can simply copy the format you need and use it in your design or code.

Fine-Tune Colors with Built-in Picker

If the picked color isn’t exactly what you want, you can adjust it using the built-in color picker. You can change things like hue, brightness, or transparency until it looks right. It gives you a bit more control without making things complicated.

Generate a Color Palette from Image

The tool can also create a color palette based on your image. It pulls out the main colors and shows them together, which is helpful when you’re trying to build a consistent design or match colors easily.

Export and Use Colors Anywhere

After selecting your colors, you can export them in different formats like CSS code, SVG, or even as an image. This makes it easy to use your colors directly in projects without extra steps.

Simple Tool for Everyday Use

Whether you’re working on design, coding, or just exploring colors, this tool keeps things simple. You can quickly pick, copy, and use colors without overthinking the process.
